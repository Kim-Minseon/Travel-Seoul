🏯 Explore Seoul

Things To Do

From ancient palaces to frozen beer and photo booths

Palace · Free w/ Hanbok

Gyeongbokgung Palace 경복궁

The grandest of Seoul's five palaces. Arrive early for the guard changing ceremony (10am & 2pm). Rent a hanbok at the gate — get free admission while wearing it.

🕘 9am–6pm (closed Tue) 💰 ₩3,000 🚇 Gyeongbokgung (Line 3)
Maps →
Free

Deoksugung Stone Wall Walk 돌담길 야경

The illuminated stone wall of Deoksugung Palace at night is one of Seoul's most atmospheric walks. Perfect after dinner — 15 min from City Hall station.

🌙 Best after 7pm 💰 Free 🚇 City Hall (Lines 1 & 2)
Maps →
Free

Han River at Night 한강 야경

Grab convenience store snacks and join locals at Banpo Hangang Park. The Moonlight Rainbow Fountain runs 9–11pm weekends. Bring a mat and sit by the water.

🌙 Best after dark 💰 Free 🚇 Express Bus Terminal (Line 3)
Maps →

Itaewon 이태원

International neighbourhood with global restaurants, craft cocktail bars, vintage boutiques, and lively nightlife. Great for a break from Korean food, or when you want to meet other travellers.

🚇 Itaewon (Line 6) 💰 Varies
Maps →

Seongsu Pop-Up District 성수 팝업스토어

Seoul's Brooklyn — former industrial district packed with rotating pop-up stores, specialty coffee, and local creative brands. New things appear every week.

🚇 Seongsu (Line 2) 💰 Free to browse

Specialty Cafes

Seoul takes coffee seriously. Explore Yeonnam-dong, Mangwon, or Seongsu for independent cafés with single-origin roasts and stunning interiors. ₩6,000 lattes that actually taste good.

🕘 9am–10pm usually 💰 ₩5,000–8,000
Maps →

Traditional Markets 재래시장

Gwangjang Market (광장시장) — bindaetteok pancakes and silk. Namdaemun — everything imaginable. Mangwon Market — local, relaxed, less touristy.

🕘 Morning is best 💰 Free entry

K-Culture Must

Photo Booths 인생네컷

Korea's coin photo booth craze — self-service booths with beautiful filters and instant prints. Chains to try:

📸 인생네컷 (Life 4 Cuts) 🎞️ 포토그레이 (Photogray) 🎬 하루필름 (Haru Film) 🖤 모노맨션 (Mono Mansion)

4 prints for ~₩4,000–6,000. Found in every mall, shopping district, and subway station.

Maps →

Norebang (Karaoke) 노래방

Private karaoke rooms rented by the hour. Tambourines, disco lights, unlimited songs. A quintessential Korean night. For solo: look for coin-operated 코인노래방.

🕐 Open until 5am+ 💰 ₩10,000–20,000/hr
Maps →
Open 24h

PC Bang PC방

Korea's legendary gaming cafes — top-tier gaming PCs, 1Gbps internet, and you can order food to your seat. Even if you don't game, it's a uniquely Korean experience.

🕐 Open 24 hours 💰 ~₩1,500/hr
Maps →

Korean Drinking Culture 술 문화

Soju (소주) + beer = somaek (소맥). Try a pojangmacha (포장마차) tent bar for the most authentic setting — plastic chairs, grilled skewers, cheap drinks.

🍶 Soju: ₩1,500 at GS25 🍺 Bar beer: ₩3,000–6,000
Maps →
Summer Exclusive

Frozen Beer 얼음맥주

Beer served in a frozen mug with ice crystals forming on the surface. Ask for "얼음맥주" at any hof (호프) bar. Life-changing on a hot, humid July day in Seoul.

🌡️ Perfect for July heat 💰 ₩4,000–7,000
Maps →
Open 24h

Convenience Stores 편의점

CU, GS25, 7-Eleven, Emart24 — on every block, 24/7. Hot foods at the counter, ₩1,500 Americanos, soju, beer, kimbap, and anything you forgot to pack. A Korean lifestyle staple.

🕐 24/7 💰 Budget heaven
🍜 Korean Cuisine

Korean Food Guide

Must-try dishes. Tap the Korean name to copy it — paste into Maps or show your server.

Samgyeopsal

Thick-cut pork belly grilled at the table. Wrap in lettuce with garlic, green peppers, and ssamjang. Korea's most beloved BBQ.

Sutbul Galbi

Charcoal-grilled short ribs with smoky, caramelized edges. A step above regular BBQ — worth every won.

₩30,000–50,000 / 인 Find on Maps →
Bulgogi

Thinly sliced marinated beef, sweet and savory. Grilled or stir-fried — one of Korea's most internationally recognized dishes.

₩15,000–20,000 / 인 Find on Maps →
Hanwoo Beef

Premium Korean native cattle — extraordinary marbling. More expensive but genuinely life-changing. The wagyu of Korea.

₩60,000–120,000+ / 인 Find on Maps →
Dakgalbi

Spicy stir-fried chicken with rice cakes, cabbage, and gochujang on a hot iron plate. Add extra cheese for the modern version.

₩13,000–17,000 / 인 Find on Maps →
Bossam

Slow-boiled pork belly wrapped in cabbage or perilla leaves with oyster kimchi and radish. Mild, tender, endlessly satisfying.

₩15,000–25,000 / 인 Find on Maps →
Jokbal

Braised pig's trotters in soy sauce, ginger, and spices. Gelatinous, tender, and surprisingly delicious dipped in salted shrimp paste.

₩20,000–30,000 / 인 Find on Maps →
Jeyuk Bokkeum

Spicy stir-fried pork with gochujang. A weekday lunch staple — served over rice with banchan side dishes.

₩8,000–12,000 Find on Maps →
Kimchi Jjigae

Kimchi stew with pork and tofu. Korea's ultimate comfort food — best with well-fermented kimchi. Served bubbling hot at the table.

₩8,000–12,000 Find on Maps →
Doenjang Jjigae

Fermented soybean paste stew with tofu, zucchini, and mushrooms. Korea's answer to miso soup — earthier, heartier, deeply umami.

₩7,000–10,000 Find on Maps →
Budae Jjigae

"Army Stew" — spam, hot dogs, ramen, kimchi, and cheese in spicy broth. Born after the Korean War, still iconic decades later.

₩11,000–16,000 / 인 Find on Maps →
Gukbap

Rice in hot pork or beef bone broth, loaded with meat. Korea's ultimate hangover cure and the perfect late-night meal. Open 24 hours.

₩7,000–10,000 Find on Maps →
Naejang Tang

Intensely flavored spicy offal soup. A bold local favorite — not for the squeamish, but a deeply authentic Korean experience.

₩8,000–12,000 Find on Maps →
Jjajangmyeon

Noodles in black bean sauce with pork and vegetables. Korea's #1 delivery food — rich, satisfying, nothing like Chinese lo mein.

₩6,000–8,000 Find on Maps →
Jjamppong

Spicy red seafood noodle soup. The fiery counterpart to jjajangmyeon — often ordered together as a combo at Chinese-Korean restaurants.

₩7,000–9,000 Find on Maps →
Tangsuyuk

Korean sweet-and-sour crispy pork. Ordered alongside jjajangmyeon. The great debate: dip (찍먹) or pour sauce (부먹)? Pick a side.

₩18,000–25,000 (공유) Find on Maps →
Kalguksu

Handmade knife-cut wheat noodles in a clear anchovy or chicken broth. Simple, hearty, and deeply comforting after a long session day.

₩8,000–12,000 Find on Maps →
Bibim Guksu

Spicy cold mixed noodles with gochujang, cucumber, and egg. Refreshing and punchy — a lifesaver in Seoul's hot, humid July weather.

₩7,000–10,000 Find on Maps →
Janchi Guksu

Traditional banquet noodle soup — thin noodles in a light, clean anchovy broth. Elegant simplicity. The gentle option when your stomach needs a break.

₩6,000–8,000 Find on Maps →
Tteokbokki

Chewy rice cakes in spicy-sweet gochujang sauce. Korea's most beloved street food — sold everywhere from pojangmacha to upscale restaurants.

₩3,000–5,000 Find on Maps →
Sundae

Korean blood sausage stuffed with glass noodles and vegetables. Usually served with tteokbokki and twigim as the classic street food trio.

₩3,000–5,000 Find on Maps →
Twigim

Korean-style fried snacks — shrimp, squid, sweet potato, and more. Sold by weight at street stalls, typically dipped in tteokbokki sauce.

₩1,000–3,000 Find on Maps →
Gimbap

Seaweed rice rolls with egg, vegetables, and fillings. The perfect on-the-go meal between sessions — at every 분식 shop and convenience store.

₩3,000–5,000 Find on Maps →
Korean Fried Chicken

Double-fried for maximum crunch. Three styles: Original (후라이드), sweet-spicy sauce (양념), soy garlic (간장). Order with icy cold beer — "치맥"!

₩18,000–25,000 Find on Maps →
Hoe (Raw Fish)

Korean-style sashimi — fresh fish served with sesame oil & salt, or wrapped in perilla with gochujang. Distinctly different from Japanese sashimi.

₩30,000–70,000+ / 인 Find on Maps →
Maeuntang

Fiery spiced fish stew with tofu, mushrooms, and vegetables. Traditionally served after 회 — the hot broth made from the remaining fish.

₩10,000–20,000 Find on Maps →
Chobap (Korean Sushi)

Korean-style nigiri with fresh domestic fish. Larger portions, competitive quality, and usually better value than you'd expect.

💡 Local Knowledge

Insider Tips

Things locals know that guidebooks don't always cover

💳

T-Money Card (T머니)

Buy at any convenience store for ₩4,000, load cash at subway machines. Tap on/off buses, subways, and most taxis. Saves you from buying individual tickets every time.

🗺️

Use Naver Maps, Not Google

Google Maps has poor transit routing in Korea — bus schedules are often wrong. Naver Maps (네이버 지도) has an English UI and real-time bus tracking. Download before you land.

🚕

KakaoT for Taxis (카카오T)

Shows fare upfront, in-app card payment, shareable ride link. Essential since most drivers don't speak English and Korean addresses can be confusing.

💰

Cash vs. Card

Most restaurants accept cards. Street food stalls prefer cash. ATMs are everywhere — look for Woori Bank or Shinhan Bank "Global ATM" for foreign cards without extra fees.

🌡️

July Heat & Humidity

Expect 28–35 °C (82–95 °F) with high humidity. The subway and COEX are heavily air-conditioned — bring a light layer. Convenience stores sell cold drinks for ₩1,000–2,000.

🗣️

Useful Korean Phrases

"이거 주세요" (igeo juseyo) — "This one, please."
"얼마예요?" (eolmayeyo) — "How much?"
"감사합니다" (gamsahamnida) — "Thank you."
Even a small attempt goes a long way.

📶

SIM Card & Pocket WiFi

Get a data SIM or pocket WiFi at the Incheon Airport arrivals hall. Korea has excellent 5G everywhere. A 10-day SIM is ~₩20,000–30,000.

🕐

Break Time (브레이크타임)

Many restaurants close for break time from ~3–5pm. If the door is locked mid-afternoon, check the posted hours. Convenience stores are your friend during these gaps.

🥢

Dining Etiquette

Wait for elders to eat first. Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice. Eating from shared dishes is normal. Slurping noodles is fine — it shows you're enjoying the food.
