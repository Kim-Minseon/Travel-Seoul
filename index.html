Things To Do in Seoul

From ancient palaces to frozen beer — Seoul has something for every evening

🏯

Gyeongbokgung Palace 경복궁

The grandest of Seoul's five palaces. Arrive early for the changing of the guard ceremony (10am & 2pm). Rent a hanbok at the gate — you get free admission while wearing it.

🕘 9am–6pm (closed Tue) 💰 ₩3,000 🚇 Gyeongbokgung (Line 3)

Deoksugung Stone Wall Walk 돌담길 야경

The illuminated stone wall of Deoksugung Palace at night is one of Seoul's most atmospheric walks. Perfect after dinner — 15 minutes from City Hall station.

🌙 Best after 7pm 💰 Free 🚇 City Hall (Lines 1 & 2)

Han River at Night 한강 야경

Korean Drinking Culture 술 문화

Soju (소주) + beer = somaek (소맥), Korea's go-to combo. Try a pojangmacha (포장마차) tent bar for the most atmospheric setting — plastic chairs, grilled skewers, cheap drinks.

🍶 Soju: ₩1,500 at convenience store 🍺 Beer: ₩3,000–6,000 at bars
Maps →
🧊

Frozen Beer 얼음맥주

One of Seoul's summer secrets — beer poured over a frozen mug with ice crystals forming on the surface. Ask for "얼음맥주" at any hof (호프) bar or street vendor. Life-changing in July heat.

🌡️ Perfect for July 💰 ₩4,000–7,000
Maps →

Convenience Stores 편의점

CU (씨유), GS25, 7-Eleven, Emart24 — on every block, open 24/7. Hot foods at the counter, ₩1,500 Americanos, soju, beer, kimbap, and anything you forgot to pack. A lifestyle, not just a store.

Insider Tips

Things locals know that guidebooks don't always cover

💳

T-Money Card (T머니)

Buy at any convenience store for ₩4,000, then load cash at the subway ticket machines. Tap on/off buses, subways, and most taxis. Saves the hassle of buying individual tickets every time.

🗺️

Use Naver Maps, Not Google

Google Maps has poor transit routing in Korea — the bus schedules are often wrong. Naver Maps (네이버 지도) has an English UI and real-time bus tracking. Download it before you land.

🚕

KakaoT for Taxis (카카오T)

Korea's taxi app — shows fare upfront, in-app card payment, and you can share your ride with a friend. Essential since most drivers don't speak English and addresses can be confusing.

💰

Cash vs. Card

Most restaurants and shops accept cards. Street food stalls prefer cash. ATMs are everywhere — look for Woori Bank or Shinhan Bank ATMs labeled "Global ATM" for foreign cards without extra fees.

🌡️

July Heat & Humidity

Expect 28–35°C (82–95°F) with high humidity. Carry a small towel. Convenience stores sell cold drinks for ₩1,000–2,000. The subway and COEX are heavily air-conditioned — bring a light layer.

🗣️

Useful Korean Phrases

"이거 주세요" (igeo juseyo) — "This one, please." Point and say it.
"얼마예요?" (eolmayeyo) — "How much?"
"감사합니다" (gamsahamnida) — "Thank you."
Even a small attempt will go over well.

📶

SIM Card & Pocket WiFi

Get a data SIM or pocket WiFi at the Incheon Airport arrivals hall — just walk out and the booths are right there. Korea has excellent 5G coverage everywhere in Seoul. A 10-day SIM is ~₩20,000–30,000.

🕐

Break Time (브레이크타임)

Many Korean restaurants close for a "break time" from ~3–5pm between lunch and dinner. If you show up and the door is locked mid-afternoon, check the hours posted on the door. Convenience stores are your friend.

🥢

Dining Etiquette

Wait for elders or senior colleagues to eat first. Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice. Eating from shared dishes is perfectly normal. Slurping noodles is fine — it shows you're enjoying the food.
